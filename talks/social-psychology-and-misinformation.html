TEDxNapierBridge | TruthCon: Tackling Misinformation | 2024, Media, News, Psychology, Social Change, Social Interaction

Is social psychology key to tackle misinformation?

Understanding our own cognitive biases is paramount to prepare ourselves to face the current misinformation pandemic, says Dr. Sindhuja Sankaran, a social psychologist. Learn simple ways to identify when you or your peer group might be susceptible to misinformation thereby being able to be better informed and unbiased.

Dr. Sindhuja Sankaran

Dr. Sindhuja Sankaran is a social psychologist who graduated from Cardiff University, UK and has previously worked with Jagiellonian University and the University of Warsaw in Poland. She is a founding member of the psychology department at Sai University. She is an expert in the field of social cognition, specifically on cognitive and motivational processes predicting individual and group behavior. Dr. Sindhuja founded her NGO ‘Rethinking Refugees’ in 2018. This talk was given at a TEDx event using the TED conference format but independently organized by a local community.
